Budget Travel Tips Video

What You'll Learn
You'll see many different dishes from Lima, Peru. Furthermore, you'll discover common fruits and desserts in Lima that you may not have encountered before. The video showcases dishes from all parts of Peru, as many Peruvians from different regions migrate to Lima for better opportunities, creating a melting pot of traditions from across the country.
Why Watch?
This video offers practical advice to make your trip enjoyable without overspending. You'll discover the wide variety of food and beverages you can buy, especially at markets with many family-owned restaurants. There are numerous restaurants in the markets, as shown in this video.

My best advice!

Peru has affordable dining at every corner...

Look for local "menú del día" options at small restaurants or food stands. The food is usually fresh, but if it looks stale, trust your instincts. Additionally, your stomach may need a couple of days to adjust, but after that, you'll be able to enjoy delicious food at an amazing price.

Meals Price Comparison

Meal Type Budget Option Mid-Range Option Expensive Option
Breakfast $2-3 (Street food/cafes) $5-8 (Casual restaurants) $15+ (Hotel dining or upscale cafes)
Lunch $3-5 ("Menu del día") $10-15 (Casual dining) $20+ (Upscale restaurants)
Dinner $3-5 (food stands/ soup kitchen) $10+ (polleria) $30+ (Fine dining)
